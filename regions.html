Region I - Ilocos Region

Vigan City, Ilocos Sur

Vigan City is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its well-preserved Spanish colonial architecture, blending Asian and European influences seamlessly. Established in the 16th century as a trading port, it offers a unique glimpse into the past with its cobblestone streets and traditional bahay na bato homes. Highlights include Calle Crisologo, lined with vintage stone houses, the iconic Bantay Bell Tower providing panoramic views, and traditional pottery making that preserves the region's heritage. Vigan stands as a testament to the fusion of diverse cultures throughout its history.

Pagudpud, Ilocos Norte

Known as the "Boracay of the North," Pagudpud boasts stunning white-sand beaches and crystal-clear waters that have gained popularity over recent decades. This hidden gem showcases a perfect blend of natural beauty and tranquility, with attractions like Saud Beach offering pristine shores and gentle waves for relaxation. The Kapurpurawan Rock Formation captivates visitors with its unique limestone formations, while Bangui Windmills stand tall against the backdrop of the sea, providing a striking sight against the landscape. Pagudpud is a haven for those seeking an escape to nature’s untouched beauty.

Region II - Cagayan Valley

Batanes Islands

Known for its dramatic landscapes, stone houses, and strong cultural identity, Batanes is a hidden gem that offers an intimate experience of the Filipino way of life. Its rolling hills, jagged coastlines, and traditional Ivatan architecture create a unique atmosphere, making it a perfect destination for those seeking tranquility and a deeper connection with nature.

Region III - Central Luzon

Tarlac's Mt. Pinatubo

Tarlac’s Mt. Pinatubo is famous for its dramatic landscape, shaped by a cataclysmic eruption in 1991. Its turquoise crater lake and rugged terrain attract trekkers and adventurers seeking a breathtaking experience amidst volcanic remnants.

Region IV-A - CALABARZON

Taal Volcano, Batangas

The world’s smallest active volcano, Taal Volcano, captivates visitors with its unique structure of a lake within a crater. Offering boat rides and panoramic views from Tagaytay, Taal Volcano is a destination that merges geological wonder with scenic natural beauty. Hiking trails and breathtaking vistas provide a memorable experience, drawing tourists year-round.

Pagsanjan Falls, Laguna

Pagsanjan Falls is a popular destination featuring a three-tiered waterfall, surrounded by lush greenery. Canoe rides and the stunning natural surroundings make it a historic destination that continues to attract both locals and tourists seeking adventure and a connection with nature.

Region IV-B - MIMAROPA

Puerto Princesa, Palawan

The Puerto Princesa Subterranean River National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the New 7 Wonders of Nature, is located in Palawan. It features an 8.2-kilometer underground river that flows directly into the sea, showcasing stunning limestone karst formations, cave systems, and diverse wildlife. Visitors can explore the river on boat tours, marveling at the natural rock formations and the serene atmosphere.

Region V - Bicol Region

Mayon Volcano, Albay

Mayon Volcano is a natural wonder with its perfectly symmetrical cone shape, making it one of the most photographed landscapes in the Philippines. An active volcano, it has witnessed eruptions since 1616, and its surrounding areas like Cagsawa Ruins Park and scenic hiking trails offer adventure and unparalleled views. ATV rides and nature tours allow tourists to experience the rugged beauty of this volcanic paradise safely, leaving them in awe of its sheer power and beauty.

Region VI - Western Visayas

Boracay Island, Aklan

Boracay is a world-renowned beach destination, celebrated for its powdery white sand and vibrant nightlife. Known for its stunning sunsets, crystal-clear waters, and water sports activities, it has been a tropical paradise since the 1980s. With its bustling White Beach and diverse dining and entertainment options, Boracay offers a complete vacation experience for both relaxation and adventure.

Region VII - Central Visayas

Chocolate Hills, Bohol

The Chocolate Hills are a unique geological formation resembling chocolate mounds during the dry season, earning their status as a national geological monument. ATV rides and viewing decks provide visitors with stunning views, making it a popular tourist destination for nature lovers and those fascinated by geological wonders.

Oslob, Cebu

Famous for whale shark encounters, Oslob has become an eco-tourism hub. Visitors can experience snorkeling with whale sharks, as well as explore nearby attractions like Tumalog Falls and Sumilon Island, all offering a chance to immerse in Cebu’s natural beauty.

Region VIII - Eastern Visayas

San Juanico Bridge, Leyte-Samar

The San Juanico Bridge is the longest bridge in the Philippines, symbolizing unity between Leyte and Samar. Scenic views and cultural exhibits along the bridge make it a noteworthy destination for history buffs and travelers alike.

Kawasan Falls, Cebu

Kawasan Falls is a three-tiered waterfall featuring turquoise waters, surrounded by lush greenery. A favorite spot for canyoneering and bamboo raft rides, it provides a refreshing escape into nature.

Region IX - Zamboanga Peninsula

Great Sta. Cruz Island, Zamboanga

Great Sta. Cruz Island is renowned for its pink sand beaches, formed by crushed red organ-pipe coral. Eco-tours exploring marine life and cultural heritage make it a unique destination in Zamboanga.

Region X - Northern Mindanao

Camiguin Island

Known as the "Island Born of Fire," Camiguin is dotted with numerous volcanic craters, hot springs, and natural wonders. The White Island sandbar and Sunken Cemetery are popular attractions, providing visitors with memorable experiences in a serene, volcanic landscape.

Region XI - Davao Region

Mount Apo, Davao del Sur

Mount Apo, the tallest mountain in the Philippines, is a natural park offering rich biodiversity. Trekking through its trails allows adventurers to encounter diverse flora and fauna, with stunning views at every turn.

Samal Island

A tropical paradise near Davao City, Samal Island is home to Pearl Farm Beach Resort and Hagimit Falls, offering relaxation and adventure amidst coastal beauty.

CARAGA Region

Siargao Island, Surigao del Norte

The surfing capital of the Philippines, Siargao Island is famous for Cloud 9, a renowned surf spot attracting surfers from around the world. Island hopping, lagoon explorations, and its vibrant marine life make it a top destination for adventure enthusiasts.

Enchanted River, Surigao del Sur

Enchanted River is a crystal-clear, mysterious river with striking blue waters, surrounded by lush landscapes. It’s a place shrouded in local legends and offers activities like swimming, boat rides, and fish feeding.
